## Before You Start

<Aside>
Make sure the sound system is on — see [Turning the Sound System On](/documentation/audio/turning_sound_system_on/).
Then open the wall touchscreen and select the correct preset — see [Using Wall Audio Controls](/documentation/audio/wall_audio_controls/).

- Use **`Pulpit Wired Mics`** for channels `Pulpit 1` and `Pulpit 2`.
- Use **`Wireless Mics`** for `Wireless 1`, `Wireless 2`, and `Lapel`.
</Aside>

## Microphone Setup

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Pulpit Microphone">
    <Steps>
    1. Locate the pulpit microphone and cable, if not already plugged in.
    2. Plug the microphone into `Pulpit 1` or `Pulpit 2`.
    3. On the wall controls, open `Pulpit Wired Mics`.
    4. If the selected channel shows `Muted` in red, press once to unmute.
    5. Set the level with `+1%`, `-1%`, or the slider.
    </Steps>

    ![Pulpit Microphone](/assets/images/audio/microphones/new-mics/pulpit.jpeg)

    ![Pulpit Input Plugs](/assets/images/audio/microphones/new-mics/pulpit-plugs.jpeg)
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Lapel (Wireless)">
    <Steps>
    1. Locate the lapel bodypack and lapel mic (kept in the pulpit when not in use).
    2. Move the top power switch to `on`.
    3. Clip the lapel mic and begin speaking.
    4. On the wall controls, open `Wireless Mics` and use channel `Lapel`.
    5. If channel `Lapel` shows `Muted` in red, press once to unmute.
    6. Set the level with `+1%`, `-1%`, or the slider.
    </Steps>

    <Steps>
    1. Locate `Handheld 1` (kept in the pulpit when not in use).
    2. Short-press `ON/OFF` to turn on. Press and hold `ON/OFF` to turn off.
    3. Use the handheld mute switch to mute or unmute.
    4. On the wall controls, open `Wireless Mics` and use channel `Wireless 1`.
    5. If channel `Wireless 1` shows `Muted` in red, press once to unmute.
    6. Set the level with `+1%`, `-1%`, or the slider.

## Mute and Level Notes

- A red `Muted` button means that channel is muted — press once to unmute.
- Use `Default` to return a channel to its normal level.
- Press and hold `+1%` or `-1%` for faster continuous changes.
- Start with lower levels, speak into the mic, then raise gradually.

<Aside type="caution">
If a wireless mic has no audio, check the battery level and the power switch position.
</Aside>
